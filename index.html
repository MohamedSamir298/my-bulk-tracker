💪 Key Gym Exercises for Posture
Face Pulls
🔥 TOP PRIORITY
Best exercise for rear delts + external rotators. Counteracts every press you do.
How: Cable at head height, rope attachment. Pull toward face, elbows flaring out, hands ending beside ears. Pause 1 sec. Go light — 4×20.
Day 2 — 4×20
Rear Delt Machine Fly
🔥 TOP PRIORITY
Added to Day 1 specifically for your posture. Balances all the pressing with a direct rear delt hit — the muscle most responsible for pulling shoulders back.
How: Set arms at shoulder height, squeeze rear delts at the end of each rep. Don't use momentum. 3×15 light weight, full squeeze.
Day 1 — 3×15
Chin Tucks
🔥 FORWARD HEAD FIX
The single best exercise for forward head posture. Reactivates deep cervical flexors.
How: Pull chin straight back without nodding (double chin motion). Hold 5 sec. Release. 3×15 daily. Feel the stretch at base of skull.
Daily — morning routine
Chest-Supported Machine Row
🔥 TOP PRIORITY
Replaces bent-over row — same back stimulus with zero lower back involvement. Full focus on scapular retraction.
How: At end of each rep, squeeze shoulder blades together AND pull them down away from ears. Hold 2 sec. 4×8.
Day 2 — 4×8
Band Pull-Aparts
DAILY WARM-UP
Activates mid-traps and rhomboids before every session. Takes 60 seconds.
How: Hold band at shoulder height, arms straight. Pull apart until band touches chest. Squeeze shoulder blades. 3×20 before every session.
Pre-workout warm-up — every day
Hyperextensions
SUPPORTING
Strengthens erector spinae and glute tie-in. Keeps your lower back strong to support the upper back posture work.
How: Lower slowly, squeeze glutes at the top. Don't hyperextend past neutral. 3×12.
Day 6 — 3×12
🧘 Daily Stretches
Doorway Chest Stretch
Forearms on door frame, step through gently. Opens pec minor — most responsible for rounding shoulders forward.
60 sec × 2 · After every Push session
Suboccipital Release
Interlace fingers behind skull at base of neck. Apply gentle upward pressure. Breathe deeply. Releases tight muscles pulling head forward.
90 sec · Every morning
Levator Scapulae Stretch
Tilt ear toward shoulder, rotate chin down 45°, gentle hand pressure. Each side. Always tight in tall people.
45 sec each side · Daily
Wall Angels
Back flat against wall, arms in goalpost. Slide up overhead keeping full contact. If you lose contact — that's your mobility deficit.
3×10 reps · Daily warm-up
Deep Neck Flexor Hold
Lie flat. Tiny chin nod toward chest. Hold 10 seconds. Reactivates deep neck flexors switched off by forward head posture.
10 sec × 10 reps · Daily
